channels are a communication mechanism that allow us to connect concurrent goroutines. 
Channels can be:
Unbuffered - the sender goroutine blocks until the receiver goroutine is ready 
Buffered - The sender goroutine blocks only when the buffer is full 

G1
|   -> G3
G2

G1 and G2 are parallel, all are concurrent. 

G1 and G2 need to communicate/synchronize with each other via mutexes

Concurrent goroutines have to coordinate and orchestrate. Eg, G3 needs to aggregate results from both G1 and G2. This coordination is done in channels. 

Channels can also be used to communicate when a resource ownership can be transfered. 

Mutexes when we want to share a state or access a shared resource, it ensures exclusive access to this resources. 

Channels are for signaling with or without data. Used for Coordination or ownershitp transfer should be achieved via channels. 

In general:
Paralle -> Mutexes 
Concurrent -> channels
